# Remote Review Checklist

Use this checklist after the intake package has been logged.

## Package completeness

- Required evidence items are marked `present`, `missing`, or `not_applicable`.
- Any missing required item has an owner and a follow-up action.
- Submitted files are current enough to reflect existing site conditions.

## Identity and consistency

- Property address is consistent across intake notes, appraisal, and photos.
- Home type appears consistent with the requested service.
- Jurisdiction and expected engineer licensing path are identified.

## Foundation review

- Foundation system appears to be permanent and made of durable materials.
- Support conditions are visible enough to describe the support system.
- Anchorage cues are visible or otherwise reasonably inferred from submitted evidence.
- Wheels, axles, and towing hitches appear removed or the package explains their status.
- No obvious distress suggests movement, rotation, or inadequate support.

## Site and environmental review

- Grade and drainage around the home appear acceptable for remote certification.
- No visible sign of undermining, washout, or standing water threatens supports.
- Slope, frost, flood, or soil concerns are either absent or explained well enough for remote review.

## Accessory and scope review

- Decks, porches, carports, stairs, ramps, garages, and additions are listed.
- It is clear whether an accessory structure is attached, independent, or potentially sharing load.
- Accessory wording needed in the final letter is identified.

## Output

Choose exactly one:

- `remote_review`
- `request_more_evidence`
- `site_tech_dispatch`

Record the rationale in the case file, not just the outcome.
