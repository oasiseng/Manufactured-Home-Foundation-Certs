# Evidence Requirements

This file defines the preferred evidence package for remote manufactured-home foundation review.

## Minimum remote package

| Evidence item | Status | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Appraisal report or equivalent property summary | Required | Used for address confirmation, site context, and accessory structure awareness. |
| Exterior photos from all sides | Required | Should show skirting, grade, and any visible foundation conditions. |
| Foundation photos from multiple sides | Required | Must show support type, pier spacing cues, anchorage cues, and overall condition. |
| Crawlspace or underside photos | Required when accessible | Needed to verify support configuration, disturbance, moisture, and anchorage. |
| Notes on decks, porches, carports, garages, or additions | Required | Attached structures can change the review path. |
| Survey, site plan, plot plan, or sketch | Preferred | Helps identify slopes, setbacks, and flood or drainage concerns. |
| Manufacturer or installer information | Preferred | Useful when foundation type or system history is unclear. |
| Serial/VIN or data plate photos | Preferred | Helps confirm home identity and installation era. |

## Evidence quality standards

- Images must be current enough to reflect existing conditions.
- Photos must be bright enough to distinguish materials and support conditions.
- At least one view should show the transition from grade to skirting/foundation.
- Underside images should reveal whether the chassis is carried by durable permanent supports.
- Any inaccessible area should be called out explicitly rather than assumed.

## Automatic escalation signals

- No underside photos and no explanation for why they are unavailable
- Foundation type cannot be identified from submitted images
- Signs of settlement, erosion, undermining, or unsupported fill
- Water intrusion, standing water, or drainage problems around supports
- Attached structures that may share load with the home
- Conflicting address, parcel, or structure descriptions across the package

## Notes for clients and processors

- More photos are better than fewer photos.
- A short phone video walkthrough can be useful when still images are ambiguous.
- If a lender or AHJ already requested an in-person visit, do not force a remote-only review path.
