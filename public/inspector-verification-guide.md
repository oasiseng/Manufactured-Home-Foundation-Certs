# Inspector Verification Guide by Foundation Type

This guide provides the specific items an inspector must verify for each manufactured home foundation type, organized as pass/fail checklists. It is intended for site technicians, field inspectors, and engineers performing foundation evaluations for certification, lending, or compliance purposes.

Every inspection is non-invasive unless the engagement scope explicitly authorizes invasive work (excavation, probing, or material testing). Do not assume footing depths were physically excavated unless they were. Do not state compliance unless the evidence supports the conclusion.

---

## Universal checks (all foundation types)

These items must be verified regardless of foundation type.

| Item | Pass | Fail |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Property address matches documentation | Address on the structure, mailbox, or site matches the appraisal, case file, and lender documents | Address is inconsistent or cannot be confirmed |
| Structure identity | Home appears to be a manufactured home (built on a permanent chassis to HUD code). HUD label, data plate, or serial/VIN is visible or documented | Structure cannot be confirmed as a manufactured home, or appears to be a modular or site-built home |
| Leveling | Home appears level. Doors and windows operate normally. No more than 1/4 inch difference between adjacent pier supports (24 CFR 3285.6) | Visible slope, doors binding, windows misaligned, or measured difference exceeds 1/4 inch between adjacent supports |
| Wheels, axles, and towing hitch | Removed. For FHA Title II permanent foundation classification, axles, wheels, and tongue must be removed regardless of foundation type. Status is documented even if scope does not require removal | Present when permanent-foundation classification is claimed, or present and undocumented |
| Drainage | Grade slopes away from the foundation on all sides, minimum 1/2 inch per foot for the first 10 feet (24 CFR 3285.203). No standing water against the foundation | Grade slopes toward the home, water pools against the foundation, or drainage is clearly inadequate |
| Site conditions | No visible erosion, undermining, unsupported fill, or slope failure threatening the foundation | Active erosion, undermining, exposed unsupported fill, or slope instability affecting foundation supports |
| Accessory structures | All attached structures (decks, porches, stairs, carports, garages, ramps, room additions) are identified and their structural independence or integration is documented | Attached structures are present but not evaluated, or their load-path relationship to the home is unclear |
| Flood zone status | Flood zone determination is consistent with the case file. If in a flood hazard area, lowest floor elevation relative to BFE is noted | Flood zone status is unknown or contradicts the FIRM panel, or the home is below BFE in a mapped flood hazard area |

## Pier and anchor system verification

| Item | Pass | Fail |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Pier material identified | Concrete block (CMU), steel jack stands, treated wood, or listed manufactured piers — material type is documented | Pier material is unidentifiable or non-standard (e.g., stacked loose bricks, untreated wood, improvised supports) |
| Pier height limits | Single-stack block piers are 36 inches or less. Double-stack (interlocking) is used from 36 to 67 inches. Piers 67–80 inches are double-stacked with cells grouted and reinforced with rebar, or have engineering documentation (24 CFR 3285.303(d)(2)). Piers above 80 inches have site-specific engineering | Single-stack block piers exceed 36 inches; piers 67–80 inches are not grouted/reinforced and have no engineering; piers above 80 inches have no engineering basis |
| Pier spacing | Consistent with manufacturer installation instructions or 24 CFR 3285.303 tables (typically 4 to 10 feet on center) | Irregular or excessive spacing with no engineering basis, or spacing clearly exceeds table maximums |
| Pier condition | Piers are plumb, intact, and bearing fully on their footings. No visible cracking, tilt, rotation, or displacement | Piers are tilted, cracked, displaced off footings, or have visible damage |
| Footings/pads | Concrete pads or footers are present under every pier. Pad size is appropriate for the soil bearing capacity. Pads are on firm, undisturbed soil | No pads under piers, pads are cracked or undersized, pads are on fill or disturbed soil, or piers bear directly on bare ground |
| Shims | Shims between pier top and I-beam are durable material (hardwood, metal, or approved material). Stacking is minimal and stable | Stacked softwood scraps, excessive shimming, or shims are loose/missing |
| Ground anchors | Auger-type ground anchors are present at required spacing per manufacturer instructions or wind zone requirements | Anchors are missing, insufficient in number, or spaced beyond allowable limits |
| Straps | Steel straps are tight, properly connected from I-beams to anchor heads, and show no significant corrosion or damage | Straps are loose, disconnected, severely corroded, or missing |
| Stabilizer plates | Stabilizer plates are present at anchor heads where required | Missing where required by installation instructions |
| Sidewall straps (Wind Zones II/III) | Vertical straps from the sidewall to ground anchors are present in Wind Zone II and III installations | Missing in a Wind Zone II or III location |
| Vapor barrier | 6-mil polyethylene covers the ground under the home (required when enclosed with skirting, except in arid regions per 24 CFR 3285.204) | No vapor barrier, or barrier is torn/displaced and ineffective |
| Ventilation | Crawlspace ventilation is adequate — vents are open and distributed per manufacturer instructions | No ventilation openings, or openings are blocked or inadequate |
| Skirting condition | Skirting (if present) is intact and does not interfere with support or anchorage components | Skirting is bearing on supports, restricting ventilation, or trapping water |

## Crawl space foundation verification

| Item | Pass | Fail |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Perimeter wall material | Poured concrete, grouted concrete block, or wood-framed pony wall on concrete/block stem wall — material is identified and documented | Wall material is unidentifiable, non-durable, or non-structural |
| Perimeter wall continuity | Wall is continuous around the full perimeter of the home with no structural gaps | Structural gaps, missing wall sections, or discontinuities in the perimeter enclosure |
| Footing present | A footing is visible or documented below the perimeter wall | No footing is visible or documented (footing cannot be assumed if not visible; note as a limitation) |
| Footing depth | Footing extends below the local frost depth, or frost-protected shallow foundation design is documented | Footing is visibly above frost line, or frost depth compliance cannot be determined (note limitation) |
| Footing reinforcement | Footing is reinforced concrete (rebar visible at edges or documented in construction records) | Footing is unreinforced, or reinforcement cannot be verified (note limitation) |
| Home-to-wall attachment | Home is mechanically connected to the perimeter wall: anchor bolts through sill plate, approved nailing strips, embedded steel straps from chassis I-beams to wet-set concrete anchors in the wall/footing, or equivalent fastening is visible and documented | Home rests on the wall without mechanical attachment, or attachment type cannot be verified |
| Interior piers | Interior support piers are present under chassis I-beams and ridge beams at required intervals. Material and condition are acceptable | Interior piers are missing, undersized, or show distress |
| Interior pier footings | Each interior pier has a footing (concrete pad or poured footer) | Interior piers are bearing directly on soil without a footing |
| Wall condition | No significant cracking, displacement, bowing, or separation in the perimeter walls | Horizontal cracking (sign of lateral pressure), significant vertical cracking, or wall is bowed or displaced |
| Vapor barrier | 6-mil polyethylene covers the crawlspace floor (except in arid regions per 24 CFR 3285.204) | No vapor barrier, or barrier is ineffective (in non-arid regions) |
| Ventilation | Wall vents are present, open, and distributed per code requirements | No ventilation, or vents are blocked |
| Crawlspace moisture | No standing water, no signs of chronic moisture accumulation, no wood rot on sills or posts | Standing water, efflorescence on walls, wood rot, mold, or signs of chronic moisture |
| Clearance | Adequate clearance for access and inspection (18 inches minimum typical) | Insufficient clearance, areas inaccessible |
| Wheels/axles/hitch removed | Removed | Present (disqualifies permanent foundation claim) |

## Slab-on-grade foundation verification

| Item | Pass | Fail |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Slab condition | Slab is intact at visible edges. No significant cracking, heaving, settlement, or displacement | Major cracks, heaved sections, or settlement visible at the slab edge or perimeter |
| Slab thickness | Slab appears adequate at visible edges (typically 4-6 inches). Thickened edge or turned-down footing is visible at the perimeter | Slab appears thin, no visible edge thickening, or signs of inadequate structural section |
| Anchorage | Anchor bolts or hold-down brackets are visible, connecting the home to the slab. Bolts are tight and in acceptable condition | No visible anchorage, or bolts are loose, missing, or corroded |
| Home-to-slab connection | Home is mechanically attached to the slab — not just resting on the surface | Home appears to sit on the slab without mechanical connection |
| Perimeter footing | Thickened slab edge or turned-down footing is visible at the perimeter, at or below frost depth where required | No footing is evident, or footing depth is clearly inadequate for frost conditions |
| Drainage | Grade slopes away from the slab on all sides. No water pooling against the slab edge | Water pooling against the slab, grade slopes toward the slab, or drainage is obstructed |
| Slab surface | No signs of water intrusion or moisture migration through the slab at accessible edges | Efflorescence, staining, or moisture at the slab surface or edges |
| Utility penetrations | Utilities enter cleanly through or under the slab. No settlement or voids around penetration points | Settlement, voids, or damage at utility penetrations |
| Wheels/axles/hitch removed | Removed | Present |
| Underside access limitation | Documented as a limitation — slab systems have no accessible underside | Inspector assumes underside conditions without documentation |

## Basement foundation verification

| Item | Pass | Fail |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Wall material | Poured concrete, grouted concrete block, or ICFs — material is identified | Wall material is unidentifiable or non-standard |
| Wall condition | No significant cracking, bowing, or displacement. Block walls show no horizontal step cracking | Horizontal cracking (lateral pressure failure), bowing, or significant displacement |
| Footing | Continuous reinforced concrete footing under basement walls, below frost depth | Footing is absent, inadequate, or above frost line |
| Home-to-wall connection | Anchor bolts or mechanical fastening connects the home at the top of the walls. Connection type and spacing are documented | No visible connection, or home rests on walls without attachment |
| Interior support columns | Steel lally columns, masonry columns, or wood posts are present where needed. Connections to the floor slab and beams above are visible and adequate | Columns are missing, undersized, or improperly connected |
| Floor slab | Poured concrete floor slab at the bottom of the basement. Adequate thickness, no significant cracking | No floor slab, or slab is cracked, heaved, or significantly deteriorated |
| Waterproofing | No signs of water intrusion through walls or floor. No efflorescence, staining, or active leaks | Active water intrusion, efflorescence, water stains, or standing water |
| Drainage | Perimeter drain tile or French drain is present (may be documented rather than visible). Sump pump is functional if present | No drainage system, or sump pump is non-functional |
| Backfill | Backfill against basement walls is stable. No settlement voids between the backfill and the walls | Settlement of backfill, exposed wall sections that should be below grade |
| Wheels/axles/hitch removed | Removed | Present |
| Egress | If basement is habitable or finished, emergency egress (windows or doors) meets code | Habitable basement with no egress |
| Structural adequacy for soil loading | Walls show no signs of failure from lateral soil pressure. No bowing, cracking, or inward displacement | Evidence of lateral pressure failure |

## Proprietary system verification

| Item | Pass | Fail |
| --- | --- | --- |
| System identified | Manufacturer name, model, and system type are identified from labels, documentation, or visual recognition | System cannot be identified — treat as ambiguous and escalate |
| Installation matches manufacturer instructions | Visible components match the manufacturer's installation drawings and specifications | Visible deviations from manufacturer instructions, or instructions are not available for comparison |
| Listing or certification | System is listed or certified by a nationally recognized testing agency. Documentation is available or referenced | No listing or certification can be confirmed |
| Anchorage | Connections, bolts, and anchoring components are installed per manufacturer specifications. Torque requirements are met where specified | Connections are loose, missing, or do not match specifications |
| Component condition | All manufactured components are in acceptable condition — no corrosion, damage, or deterioration that would affect structural capacity | Corroded, damaged, or deteriorated components |
| Do not apply generic rules | Review is based on the manufacturer's specific requirements, not on generic pier-and-anchor tables | Generic pier-and-anchor rules were applied to a proprietary system (invalid basis for evaluation) |

## Documentation standards

For every inspection, the following must be documented:

- Foundation type identified (or documented as unclear with rationale)
- Every verification item above marked as pass, fail, or not observable with explanation
- Photographic evidence: minimum of one photo per side of the home showing the foundation at grade, plus underside photos at corners and mid-span support locations
- Any inaccessible areas explicitly noted with the reason for inaccessibility
- Any limitation on the inspection scope (non-invasive, remote-only, partial access)
- The governing reference used for each conclusion (24 CFR 3285 section, PFGMH section, or manufacturer specification)
